My Hobby & a few of my miniatures

On this page I will show you some of my Warhammer 40k models and give a few tips on how I achieved my finished result, for those interested in painting miniatures themselves!

This is the right side of my Dreadnought model, note the free hand work on the right armour pauldron. The Space Marine chapter I chose for my dreadnought is that of the Storm Reapers!
This is my Redemptor Dreadnought. I am very proud of this model as it is the biggest model I have painted so far in my hobbying career! From a painting perspective, black is a very hard colour to pull off. But the method I use is very simple. I first spray my models with Chaos Black, then I paint all the black areas with Abaddon Black, as the they vary in colour. After I would put a layer of Nuln Oil, which is a black shade and make sure to use a brush to absorb any excess shade that could pool in areas. Lastly, I would finish the black with a simple grey highlight on all it's edges using Eshin Grey.
This is the left side of my Dreadnought. As you can see I have used a lot of metal on this side. I want to bring your attention to the detail on the parchment scroll attached to his arm. To get the old, battered look of a worn and stained parchment, you need to rely on the shading and hightlighting. Firslty I used a light brown/dust colour called Zandri Dust. After that a shade of a reddish/brown, Seraphim Sepia and then the hightlight was achieved by dry brushing Tyrant Skull. After this was all done, I worked on the details written on the scroll. This was achieved by making a custom mix of black and the reddish/brown shade i used on the scroll itself.
This is another model I have painted, my Outrider Bike. I am most pleased with how I did the hightlighting and detailing on this model. The best tip I can give here is, to make sure you match the colours you use appropriately. This is to make sure you match the base colour with the right hightlight colour.
As you can see this is the rear veiw of the Outrider model. Here I just want to point out the shading I did on the bikers exhausts. I used a method of layering shades to create a burnt metal affect, to give the impression that the exhausts are hot!
